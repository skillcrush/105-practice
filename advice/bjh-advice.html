Hey, Geek!

(Takes one to know one, right?)

So, you’re on your way to your brand new career and you’re just bubbling over with excitement.

… Well, maybe not bubbling. After all, there’s that queasiness you just can’t seem to fix — not with digestive remedies, anyway.

Been there.

My name is BJ Hahn. We haven’t met yet, but we’re headed in the same direction. Only I’ve changed direction a few times before now, and I can tell you it’s always the same.

Here’s the one thing I keep top-of-mind anytime I chart a new course. (It’s my version of avoiding panic and always carrying a towel.)

Do what you’ve always done, get what you’ve always gotten.

Discomfort and confusion are temporary. They’re just part and parcel of stepping out of your comfort zone.

And let’s face it: You can’t accomplish anything new and different if you don’t step out of your comfort zone. No matter what you’ve been doing, it’s become comfortable. … Or, at least stable. … Maybe uncomfortably stable. Let’s just say you could probably do it on autopilot.

But now, you’ve gotta think about everything.

… EVERYTHING!

And it’s scary.

And it’s seriously inconvenient.

And there’s no avoiding any of it.

Still, it’s pretty exciting too, isn’t it?

That simultaneous excitement and queasiness you’re experiencing is exactly what tells you you’re on the right course.

And as long as you don’t give up, this new and different world will eventually become your new comfort zone.

Getting there is simply a matter of perseverance and trusting the learning process.
