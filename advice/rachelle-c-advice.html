Just Keep Swimming

If you've seen the beloved children's movie Finding Nemo, you're familiar with his father Marlin's scatterbrained sidekick's mantra, "Just Keep Swimming." It's this advice that helps Marlin reach his goal of finding his son. This mantra has been a favorite of my family's since we saw the movie. No matter the sharks that try to eat you, the currents that try to push you off course, or a maze of jellyfish you have to navigate, if you keep swimming, there will always be guides along the way to help you reach your goal.

If at first you don't fricasee, fry, fry a hen

This phrase, obviously a take on "If at first you don't succeed..." made up by a quirky teenager in one of my favorite books, Laddie, is also a favorite mantra at our house. A few years ago, a friend of mine was teaching me to make flan, a delectable Latin American dessert. It was like second nature to him, so he tried to write the recipe down for me. I attempted it several times and it never turned out the way it was supposed to. Each time, I dumped it and started over. Finally I called him over to help. He laughed and said, "Don't throw that away! You've made ..." (I forget what he called it). So, as I learned from Laddie and from my friend, when you try something new and it doesn't turn out like it's supposed to, keep trying, learn from it, and see if there's something else you can use it for!
