Hi, my name is Ania and I have some advice for you!

Don't be too hard on yourself

When you start programming it can be very owerwhelming and you might get stressed by the amout of new things you have to learn. I was so stressed that I felt a lot of guilt when I wasn't learning every day after work.

Now I know that balance is very important. With learning too much I got very stressed and tired but I noticed that when I take a break and relax sometimes I get the best ideas or solutions to the problems. So don't be too hard on yourself!

Don't look too much into 'Frontend Developer Roadmap Graphs

At the beginning everything was very new and I was often searching on the internet for a roadmap to become Frontend Developer to see what skills I need to learn. Unfortunately the graphs were huge and they really owerwhelmed me and triggered my imposter syndrome.

Now that I work as a frontend Developer I know you don't necessery need all the skills from the start. Some of them are already outdated, some can be learned in quite short amount of time and some are just not used at my company. I feel that it's more important to have strong foundations than know just a little of every tool possible. The more you work as a developer the faster you learn new tools anyway.
